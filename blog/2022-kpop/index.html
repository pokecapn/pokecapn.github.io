pokecapn's top 30+ kpop of 2022

  • 29 November 2023
  • kpop year in review

(the rules)

  1. i listened to over 1500 songs, to bring you
  2. 30 that Kick Ass per my exacting standards for this list, "awards" for 10 more songs that weren't making the cut, and another 125 i found good or at least entertaining in a bonus playlist
  3. no year end lists tinted these judgments. all opinions are my own. probably
  4. original air date: November 29, 2023

previous years: 2017 | 2018 | 2019 | 2020 | 2021

awards

The Best Cover(?) Award: Funtwo, Jane, AZ - aespa - Next Level (Guitar Cover)

The Literally Just "Take On Me" Award: Pentagon - Sparkling Night

The Pryda Snare Award - Coin Star: Psy - Everyday

The Pryda Snare Award - Minigame Star: Seulgi - Los Angeles

The Pryda Snare Award - Happening Star: Lapillus - Queendom

The "We Have A Taemin Track At Home" Award: KOM - Adrenaline

The "No Really, The Song Is Good Too, Honest!" Award: (tie) Kara - When I Move, Apink Bomi & Hayoung - Red Carpet

The This Sucks But I Keep Thinking About It Award: Ryeohwa feat. Mistakim - Cowboy

The Most Likely To Have Been Rejected From A Mission: Impossible Soundtrack Award: Itzy - Voltage

the list

30) NewJeans - Hype Boy

no one singing this song has installed Windows XP before, but that didn't stop them from cranking all the numbers up and trying anyway

29) Chungha - Sparkling

a smooth and refreshing twist on the 160ish bpm synthpop formula that we've seen for the past couple of years. literally refreshing! cans open! in the song!

28) Pixy - Villain

very cool and nasty. am i the only one blacking out and seeing rapid-fire pictures of cats during the choruses? just me? i should get that looked at.

27) Red Velvet - Snap Snap

i think it's aiming for a naughty 2.0, a great house thumper that SM has regularly been able to give its artists

26) Billlie - GingaMingaYo (the strange world)

by all means i should just bounce right off the singsongy nursery rhyme chorus, but the fatness of the track and chewiness of the synths keeps me coming back

25) Oh My Girl - Replay

vicious destruction coming out of nowhere. 2 and a half very short minutes of dank filth

24) Wonho - Eye On You

a no bullshit track that cruises along, with great pauses to build up and release tension, and delightful synths that crank up during the chorus. energizer

23) STAYC - Poppy

i could have held this until the 2023 list since it did get a korean version but i though-POPPY POPPY POPOPPY POPPY POPPY LOVE LOVE POPPY POPPY POPOPPY POPPY POPPY LOVE LOVE POPPY POPPY POPOPPY POPPY POPPY LOVE LOVE POPPY POPPY POPOPPY PO

22) Drippin - The One

this year's production team that shows up multiple times in the list is the tag team of TAK & Corbin at NEWTYPE, starting with this fantastic drum & bass pop-rock hybrid. a great way to start your day

21) Purple Kiss - Pretty Psycho

the philosophy for purple kiss house tracks seems to be to load up a solid foundation with random shit and it works great. beethoven, classic movie samples, and speed garage wobble, together at last

20) Nature - Limbo

if more songs could detour into 10 seconds of psytrance for no reason that'd be swell, thanks

19) woo!ah! - Rollercoaster

it's finally time to slow down and enjoy a synthwave dreamscape. CRJ energy will be channeled several times on this trip up the list

18) fromis_9 - Rewind

while we mourn the death of any wild ass concepts ever gracing fromis_9 again, we can still appreciate solid heavily replayable b sides like this

17) Limelight - Starlight

slots right into the izone gap you didn't know you had. that one meme but the guy's going "it my fiesta fr!!!!"

16) Key - Bound

yo man what you got there, sega genesis???? the fm synth bass is off the charts

15) Yena - Pretty Boys

picture-perfect 80's throwback. way too good to be trapped on an EP with no promotion

14) Nayeon - Pop!

fast-paced bass, brass, and heys. hold on a second. now just what exactly are we popping here.

13) ATEEZ - Halazia

this one's a real ride between insanely high and deep vocals, going off the rails into drum and bass and hardstyle at the end. waiting for the day that "off the rails" becomes "the rails". that'll be something

12) Le Sserafim - Blue Flame

it's simple, it's catchy, and the star of the show is the megabass 2000 watt rms in the prechorus

11) Viviz - Bop Bop!

ezaels ocsid nimmals

10) Limelight - Eye To Eye

strap in for another dreamscape. the vocals have more room to work than the other limelight track and you can just lie down and let the waves roll in

9) Ive - Love Dive

doomsday plodding processed out the goat ass. this is a compliment. i'm convinced they're going for an another brick in the wall vibe

8) Kep1er - MVSK

a relentless assault where the only constant is change. many angry basses, unintelligible synth brass, anti-drops becoming real drops, and a trip into triplet time just for fun

7) Kwon Eunbi - Croquis

pounding productivity-enhancing house. i can and have put this on repeat for an hour. 2:17 is a great advancement in "vocals as instrument"

6) Rocket Punch - Louder

this song fucks. no further explanation needed, just listen to it

5) Taeyeon - INVU

effortless emotional dreamworld. mirror-polished. you get the feeling no one was allowed to mess this one up

4) CSR - Pop? Pop!

pure sugar fresh air with a truckload of cool rhythms and sounds. the part before the third chorus is like a boast from the production team: "we could have made this absolute dogshit, but we are masters of the craft in full control"

3) WJSN Chocome - Super Yuppers!

the song: the 80s are here, again. me:

2) Cherry Bullet - Love In Space

what's 156 bpm, is dominated by offbeat bass stabs, and features 16th note melodies? that's right, they've finally discovered the secret formula to poppify eurobeat

1) Kwon Eunbi - Glitch

a journey into sound. discover silken vocals weaving through garage with massive build ups into chunky four on the floor, replete with the titular glitching. then get blasted by the finish. lost in chimes, you conclude: "damn"
